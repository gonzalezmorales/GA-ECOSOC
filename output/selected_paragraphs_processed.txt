1: The General Assembly, in its resolution A/RES/77/20 of 25 November 2022 on Cooperation between the United Nations and the International Criminal Police Organization (INTERPOL), emphasized the importance of optimal coordination and cooperation between the United Nations and INTERPOL in countering terrorism, with particular regard to the threat posed by the travel of foreign terrorist fighters, including those who returned or relocated, and in strengthening international efforts to ensure that refugee status was not abused by the perpetrators, organizers, and facilitators of terrorist acts, consistent with the obligations of Member States under international law. In this context, it stressed the importance of information-sharing, as appropriate, including biometric data, which could encompass fingerprints and photographs, in order to increase the chances of the positive identification of terrorists and their affiliates. It also emphasized the importance of Member States making full use of INTERPOL capabilities in this regard, notably the Stolen and Lost Travel Document Database, the Nominal Database, the Foreign Terrorist Fighter Criminal Analysis File, the Fingerprint Database, the DNA Profile Database, and the facial recognition system, and of promoting international cooperation efforts to assist Member States, upon their request, in strengthening cooperation between their law enforcement agencies to bring suspected terrorists to justice.
